Welcome to my website!

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Political Science at Stockholm University .

I study the global governance of Artificial Intelligence (AI). My research interest is driven by the observation that the rapid development of AI presents one of the most consequential governance challenges to date. Specifically, it represents a governance challenge of global scale, given the inherently trans-boundary nature of AI’s development and deployment. In my dissertation, I investigate various cooperation efforts to facilitate AI governance at the global and regional levels of governance.

Methodologically, I mainly rely on large-N data collection efforts for descriptive and statistical analysis, as well as quantitative text analysis.

During my doctoral studies, I had the opportunity to conduct two research visits: in spring 2024, as a departmental guest at the Niehaus Center for Globalization and Governance at Princeton University, and in fall 2024, as a Global Political Economy Project (GPEP) Affiliate Fellow at Georgetown University. I am affiliated with the Stockholm Center on Global Governance (SCGG) and am a student member of the Wallenberg AI, Autonomous Systems and Software Program – Humanity and Society (WASP-HS) graduate school.
